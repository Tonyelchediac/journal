The White House: Symbol of American Leadership

The White House is the official residence and workplace of the President of the United States. Located at 1600 Pennsylvania Avenue NW in Washington, D.C., it has served as the home of every U.S. president since John Adams in 1800. Designed by Irish-born architect James Hoban, the White House was built between 1792 and 1800 and has undergone several renovations and expansions over the centuries.
