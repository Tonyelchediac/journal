The Lebanese Presidential Palace, also known as Baabda Palace, serves as the official residence and workplace of the President of Lebanon. Located in the Baabda district southeast of Beirut, the palace is an important center for political activity and state ceremonies. Constructed in the 1960s, Baabda Palace features a blend of modern and traditional Lebanese architectural styles, surrounded by lush gardens and scenic views of the capital. The palace hosts official meetings, diplomatic receptions, and national celebrations, reflecting its role as a symbol of Lebanese sovereignty and unity.
